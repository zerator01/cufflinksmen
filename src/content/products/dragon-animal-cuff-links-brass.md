## The Detail That Changes Everything

You button the shirt. You knot the tie. Everything fits — but something is missing. That is where the dragon comes in. At 15 by 12 millimetres, the animal motif on these brass cuff links is small enough to stay refined, bold enough to start a conversation. Cast in solid brass rather than hollow alloy, the weight of the metal tells your wrist you mean business. Two finishes — gold color and silver color — let you match the rest of the wardrobe without thinking. Slide them on, and the toggle locks with a quiet click. The meeting has not started yet, but you already have their attention.

### An Animal Motif That Earns Its Place

The dragon is one of the oldest symbols in men's jewellery — power, wisdom, and a certain controlled danger. Instead of a generic enamel fill, these cuff links carry the motif as a sculpted relief on a solid brass face. The result is a piece that reads differently under different lights: a low shine in a dim restaurant, a sharp gleam under conference room halogens. At 15 by 12 millimetres, the face sits proud without overwhelming a French cuff or a single-fold cuff alike.

### Solid Brass. Not Hollow. Not Weak.

Many cuff links in this price range are hollow-cast — lightweight and prone to bending over time. These are solid brass throughout. The difference is immediate the moment you put them on: a reassuring heft that signals quality. The toggle closure mechanism, a classic bullet-back design, slides through the cuff hole and clicks into place with zero risk of slipping out mid-handshake. The plating — gold color or silver color — sits on top of that solid core without the brittleness of cheaper alternatives.

### Ready to Give. Ready to Wear.

No tissue paper, no bulk bag. These arrive in gift-ready packaging, so whether it is a birthday, a graduation, a promotion, or a just-because moment, you can hand them over without repackaging. To keep them at their best: wipe with a soft dry cloth after wearing, avoid perfume and cleaning products, and store them separately so the plating stays scratch-free. A brass accessory treated with basic care will outlast fast fashion by years.
