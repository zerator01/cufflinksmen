### Stone That Refracts Like a Prism, Not Like Glass

The round brilliant cut on each cubic zircon stone follows the same 57-facet geometry used for fine diamonds. Light enters the stone, bounces between facets, and exits as a controlled burst of brilliance — not a flat glint, but genuine optical fire. This is the difference between an accessory that looks expensive and one that looks like a costume. The stone is set flush into a copper base polished to a mirror shine, creating a warm gold surround that frames the colour rather than competing with it.

### A Clasp That Holds All Night, Not Just for the Photos

The fixed butterfly clasp — the same mechanism used in quality jewellery houses — engages with a quiet click and stays seated through handshakes, applause, and dinner service. Unlike lever-back cufflinks that can twist open under pressure, these are designed to be set once at the dressing table and forgotten. The copper post is weighted to sit correctly on the cuff without drooping or spinning, maintaining the clean horizontal line that French cuffs are built to display.
