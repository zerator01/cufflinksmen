# Quality Review SOP

Use this SOP to review storefront quality before major launches and after significant copy or catalog changes.

## Review Goals

- Catch thin or repetitive product and collection copy
- Catch broken trust pages, fake contact paths, or dead-end navigation
- Catch unsupported product, material, or policy claims
- Catch merchandising layouts that bury the next useful action

## Sampling Frame

- Review the homepage hero and one curated block
- Review at least one collection page
- Review at least one strong PDP
- Review at least one weak or edge-case PDP
- Review `contact`, `help`, and one legal or policy page
- If the site uses email capture, wholesale intake, or another lead path, review one complete path end to end

## Checks

- Headline and summary read naturally and match what the page actually offers
- Product claims do not exceed seller-provided evidence or site policy reality
- Support and policy pages contain working contact details and non-fake actions
- Related products and collection cards point users into relevant next steps
- Canonical, title, description, and indexing intent still match the route

## Escalation Conditions

- Repeated copy patterns spread across many product pages
- A route family starts producing pages that no longer justify indexing
- Trust pages degrade into placeholders, broken mailto links, or empty promises
- A copy or merch change alters category meaning without an explicit decision

## Output

- Add findings to `ops/ops-execution-log.md`
- Open remediation work grouped by `copy`, `trust`, `navigation`, `indexing`, or `merchandising`
- If the issue affects the current cycle direction, reflect it in the next-stage plan before shipping more changes
